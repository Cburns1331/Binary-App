Binary Basics

Binary is a form of counting. You know the decimal system, right? We use the digits 0 - 9 to represent amounts. We have ten fingers and ten toes, so it makes sense to count in base 10.

But computers are different. There aren't ten possible states of existence for computers; they don't have ten fingers and ten toes. Instead, computers read whether a switch has electricity or not. Electricity has only two states: on or off. This is represented as 1 (on) or 0 (off) in binary.
